About Me

I'm currently a third-year student at the University of Virginia, pursuing a Bachelor of Arts in Computer Science with an expected graduation in Spring 2026. Originally from Salt Lake City, UT, I've always had a love for the outdoors—whether it's skiing down mountain slopes or tackling trails on my mountain bike.

Since moving to Virginia, I've traded those pursuits for rowing. I'm now a dedicated member of UVA's men's rowing team, putting in about 20 hours a week for early morning practices at 6 a.m., Monday through Friday. Rowing has been an intense but rewarding experience, pushing me to new levels of persistence and dedication that have shaped who I am today.

My passion for computer science began back in middle school. Like many others, I was really into video games, but instead of just playing them, a friend and I decided to make our own. We dove headfirst into Unity 3D and C# scripting, spending countless hours working on various game projects. While none of our creations turned into the next big hit, those early efforts sparked a lasting love for coding that continues to drive me forward in my career.
